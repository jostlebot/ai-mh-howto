Building Your Knowledge Base

What is a Knowledge Base?

A knowledge base is a collection of your expertise that AI can reference. Instead of explaining your frameworks, methodologies, and voice every time, you create a foundation that Claude already "knows."

Imagine having an assistant who has read everything you've ever written and understands how you think. That's what we're building.

Step 1: Create Your Project Folder

First, let's create a dedicated space for your knowledge base.

  1. Open Terminal
  2. Run these commands:
mkdir ~/my-knowledge-base
cd ~/my-knowledge-base

You now have a folder called "my-knowledge-base" in your home directory.

Step 2: Create Your CLAUDE.md File

This special file tells Claude about you and your work. Claude Code automatically reads it.

Create the file by running Claude Code:

claude

Then ask Claude:

Create a CLAUDE.md file that I can fill in with information about myself and my work

Claude will create a template for you. Here's what to include:

CLAUDE.md Template

# About Me

## Who I Am
[Your name, title, what you do]

## My Expertise
[Your areas of specialty in mental health]

## My Frameworks
[Therapeutic approaches you use, methodologies you've developed]

## My Voice
[How you communicate — warm? clinical? conversational?]

## Common Topics I Create Content About
[List the themes you regularly address]

## Important Terms I Use
[Any specific language or concepts unique to your work]

Step 3: Add Your Content

Now add files that represent your expertise:

  • Past articles or blog posts (copy as .txt or .md files)
  • Frameworks you've developed
  • Common Q&As you answer
  • Your bio in different lengths
  • Example emails or messages that show your voice

Create a simple folder structure:

my-knowledge-base/
├── CLAUDE.md
├── frameworks/
│   └── my-main-framework.md
├── writing-samples/
│   ├── blog-post-1.md
│   └── newsletter-example.md
└── about/
    └── bio-versions.md

Step 4: Use Your Knowledge Base

Now when you run Claude Code from inside your knowledge-base folder, Claude will reference all of it.

cd ~/my-knowledge-base
claude

Try prompts like:

  • "Write a LinkedIn post about [topic] in my voice"
  • "Draft an email responding to this question: [paste question]"
  • "Create a workshop outline based on my framework"

Step 5: Keep It Growing

Your knowledge base is a living thing. As you create new content:

  1. Add new files to the appropriate folders
  2. Update your CLAUDE.md when your thinking evolves
  3. Add examples of particularly good work

The more context you provide, the better Claude can assist you.

Pro Tips

  • Quality over quantity — 10 great examples beat 100 mediocre ones
  • Include failures — add notes about what NOT to do or say
  • Be specific about voice — "I never use the word 'journey'" is helpful
  • Update regularly — your thinking evolves; your knowledge base should too

What's Next?

With your knowledge base set up, you can:

  • Generate content in your voice instantly
  • Build custom tools that understand your work
  • Share your setup with collaborators
  • Create client-facing tools that reflect your expertise

You did it! You've learned the foundations.
